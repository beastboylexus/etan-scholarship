Our Mission

The Egbert Aung Kyaing Tan Trust Scholarship exists to support Burmese students of Buddhist descent pursuing higher education in the United States.

Dr. Egbert Aung Kyaing Tan

Founder · Psychiatrist · Philanthropist

Born in Myanmar, Dr. Tan emigrated to the United States to pursue medicine.

Over 30 years, he has served communities across New York, Wisconsin, California, and Pennsylvania.

His journey from Burma to American psychiatry is the foundation of this scholarship.

The Scholarship

Eligibility

  • Of Burmese descent
  • Buddhist background or values
  • Admitted to a US college or university
  • Demonstrated financial need

Award Details

  • Amount: Determined annually
  • Duration: Per academic year
  • Covers: Tuition, living expenses, books

How to Apply

  1. 1 Check eligibility requirements
  2. 2 Prepare required documents
  3. 3 Submit your application
  4. 4 Interview with the committee
  5. 5 Award notification

Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can apply?

Any student of Burmese descent who has been admitted to a US college or university and demonstrates financial need.

How much is the scholarship?

The award amount is determined annually based on available funds and the number of qualified applicants.

When is the deadline?

Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis. Early submission is encouraged.

What documents do I need?

Proof of Burmese heritage, university admission letter, financial need statement, and two letters of recommendation.

Can I reapply if not selected?

Yes. Students may reapply in subsequent years.
